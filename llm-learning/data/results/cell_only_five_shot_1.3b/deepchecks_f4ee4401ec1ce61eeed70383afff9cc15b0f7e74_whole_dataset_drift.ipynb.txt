    ## Adding notebooks

    ### 1. Introduction to Jupyter Notebooks

    Jupyter notebooks are an open-source web application that allows you to create and share documents that contain live code, equations, visualizations, and narrative text. You can execute the code cell by cell, and the result will be displayed below the cell.

    ### 2. Creating a Jupyter Notebook

    To create a new Jupyter notebook, you can use a variety of tools, such as the Jupyter notebook itself, the Jupyter command-line interface, or a cloud-based service like Google Colab.

    ### 3. Adding Cells to a Notebook

    You can add cells to a notebook by clicking on the "+" button in the toolbar, or by pressing the "Enter" key. You can also add cells to a notebook by dragging and dropping cells from the left pane onto the right pane.

    ### 4. Editing Cells

    You can edit a cell by clicking on the cell, or by pressing the "Enter" key. You can also edit a cell by clicking on the "Edit" button in the toolbar.

    ### 5. Running Cells

    You can run a cell by clicking on the "Run" button in the toolbar, or by pressing the "Enter" key. The result of the cell will be displayed below the cell.

    ### 6. Saving and Exporting a Notebook

    You can save a notebook by clicking on the "File" menu, then selecting "Save". You can also export a notebook to a file format that is compatible with other tools, such as PDF or HTML.

    ### 7. Collaborating on a Notebook

    Jupyter notebooks are designed to be collaborative tools. You can share a notebook with others by clicking on the "Share" button in the toolbar, or by using a sharing link.
